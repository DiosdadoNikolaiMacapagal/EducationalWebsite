The Wonders of Biology

Explore the science of life in all its forms and functions.

Introduction

Biology is the study of life and living organisms, from the smallest cells to the largest ecosystems. It explores how organisms grow, reproduce, interact, and adapt to their environments, providing insights into the complexity of life on Earth.

Cells: The Building Blocks of Life

All living things are made of cells, which carry out essential functions to sustain life. Cells can be prokaryotic, like bacteria, or eukaryotic, like plants and animals. Understanding cells is fundamental to understanding biology as a whole.

Ecology and Biodiversity

Ecology studies the relationships between organisms and their environments, while biodiversity examines the variety of life across ecosystems. Protecting biodiversity is crucial for maintaining ecological balance and supporting life on Earth.

Genetics and Evolution

Genetics explores how traits are inherited and expressed through DNA. Evolution, driven by natural selection, explains how species change over time. Together, these fields help us understand the origins and diversity of life.
