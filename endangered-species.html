Endangered Species

Discover the world's most threatened species and how we can help.

Introduction

Endangered species are animals and plants that are at risk of extinction. Their survival is vital for maintaining ecological balance and biodiversity, which are essential for life on Earth.

Causes of Endangerment

The primary causes of endangerment include habitat destruction, climate change, poaching, and pollution. Human activities have accelerated these threats, pushing many species to the brink of extinction.

Why It Matters

Each species plays a unique role in its ecosystem. Losing even one can disrupt food chains and ecological balance, leading to far-reaching consequences for all living things.

Efforts to Protect Endangered Species

Conservation efforts such as habitat restoration, wildlife sanctuaries, and international treaties like CITES are crucial. Educating people and supporting sustainable practices also play a significant role.
